Health hazards

Flood damage restoration workers can experience a number of health hazards during the cleanup process. These hazards may include back, knee, and shoulder injuries from lifting heavy items. Proper lifting techniques can prevent these injuries. It is also important to remember to move bulky items in teams of two. There are also health risks associated with mold exposure. Mold can cause sneezing and runny nose, and long-term exposure can cause serious health problems. This is particularly dangerous for individuals with weakened immune systems.

Water damage can also cause infestations of pests. These pests thrive in moist environments, and they may invade your home in search of water. You may also see earwigs, drain flies, and moths in your home after a flood. You should also check your carpets and insulation for signs of infestation.
